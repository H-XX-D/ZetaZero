**Insight:** 
Incorporate a feature that actively counters confirmation bias by introducing random, unrelated information or scenarios to disrupt cognitive biases during decision-making processes. For instance, in a recommendation system, after presenting users with items based on their preferences, introduce items from completely different categories or genres to challenge their preconceived notions and broaden their perspectives. This can be implemented as an optional toggle or setting, allowing users to opt into this cognitive diversity enhancer. For example, after recommending a classic novel from the Ming Dynasty period (1368-1644), suggest a contemporary Chinese sci-fi book to encourage exploration beyond familiar territories. This approach not only enhances user engagement but also fosters a more open-minded and diverse exploration of content, reducing the risk of echo chambers.
