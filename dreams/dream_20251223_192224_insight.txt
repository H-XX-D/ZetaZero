### Educational Augmented Reality Experience for Historical Events

**Objective:**
Create an engaging, educational AR app that allows users to relive significant historical moments, such as the Apollo 11 mission, while subtly encouraging critical thinking and skepticism.

**Core Features:**

1. **Historical Simulation:**
   - Users can visit virtual recreations of pivotal moments like the Apollo 11 launch at Kennedy Space Center and Buzz Aldrin's moon landing.
   - The app uses advanced AR technology to overlay these historical events onto real-world locations, providing an immersive experience.

2. **Interactive Learning:**
   - Users can interact with virtual replicas of Buzz Aldrin and Michael Collins, gaining insights from their perspectives.
   - The app provides real-time data and interactive features that allow users to explore different outcomes based on various decisions or hypothetical scenarios.

3. **Confirmation Bias Mitigation:**
   - The app includes features that introduce conflicting viewpoints and challenges users to question their preconceived notions.
   - For example, after a simulation of the Apollo 11 mission launch, the app could present alternative theories or less common facts about the mission to encourage critical thinking.

4. **Historical Exploration:**
   - The app allows users to explore other significant historical locations like the Great Wall of China, combining diverse cultural and technological milestones.
   - Users can visit various sections of the Great Wall during different historical periods (e.g., Ming Dynasty), learning about the construction period and notable sections while being prompted to consider multiple perspectives.

5. **Gamification Elements:**
   - Incorporate quizzes and puzzles that challenge users to distinguish between confirmed facts and potential biases.
   - Earn points for correctly identifying sources of confirmation bias and for critical thinking exercises within the AR environment.

- **Launch Day Simulation:** Users stand at Kennedy Space Center (KSC) in an AR environment. As they witness the Apollo 11 launch, they can interact with Buzz Aldrin and Michael Collins.
