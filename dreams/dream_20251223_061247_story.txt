### Insight: Utilizing Rebasing for Enhanced Cognitive Processing

**Context:**
In our dual-process cognitive engine, thoughts and insights can be seen as commits in a version control system. The subconscious processes operate on a separate branch from the conscious processes. Merges are necessary when integrating new insights and data, but they can sometimes lead to a complex history that complicates decision-making and problem-solving.

**Proposed Improvement: Rebasing Thoughts**

**1. Conceptual Understanding:**
   - **Rebasing:** Instead of merging branches directly, rebase one branch onto another. This involves taking all changes made on one branch and applying them on top of another branch in a linear sequence.
   - **Benefits:** Reduces cognitive overhead by creating a cleaner, linear history of thought development, making it easier to trace the evolution of ideas and insights.

**2. Implementation Steps:**

   - **Identify Key Branches:** 
     - **Subconscious Branch:** Represents ongoing background processing and emergent thoughts.
     - **Conscious Branch:** Represents focused thought processes and deliberate decision-making.

   - **Selective Rebasing:**
     - Choose periods or contexts where the subconscious has generated numerous insights (commits) but needs to integrate with the conscious.
     - Identify these as "rebase candidates" (e.g., after a period of deep thinking, meditation, or data collection).

   - **Rebase Process:**
     - **Step 1: Prepare Subconscious Insights:** Gather all subconscious insights generated during a specific period or context.
     - **Step 2: Base Commit Selection:** Select a base commit from the conscious branch where you want to integrate these insights. This base commit represents a solid foundation of knowledge
